The perils of international travel

Entry added on 15/11/2007 by eV

During our time abroad, and on other occasions I've left the motherland. I've often wondered what the most dangerous thing is about foreign cultures and lands.
 
 I can now reveal the answer.
 
 Kerbs!
 
 Yes the most dangerous thing is that other countries have differing roadside kerb heights. Imagine the situation, you are casually walking across the road and without thinking go to step up. You take an English size step. But WAIT. This is New Zealand. Their kerbs are much higher. What follows is an ungainly and unstoppable trip, resulting in embarassment and potentially a fall.
 
 This is a problem that affects all international travellers. You have been warned
